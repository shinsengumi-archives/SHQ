Hijikata Toshizou - checking facts

by enma_o

Hello! Newbie here Glad to have joined, there seem to be such a lot of knowledgeable people around.

As such, I have a few questions, or rather a few things I'd like to have confirmed/dismissed, on what I've read so far about Hijikata's early life. (I have a special fondness for him - all the Shinsengumi boys are interesting, but this one's special!)

This is what I've found so far:
-Full name: Hijikata Toshizou Yoshitoyo. (Anyone know if he had another childhood name, or if he ever changed it later in life?)

- He was the youngest of 6 children, and had 3 brothers and 2 sisters. The younger sister's name was Onobu (she later married Sato Hikogorou). I read somewhere that the oldest brother's name was Kiroku, and the Shinsengumi! NHK series gives another brother's name as Tamajirou.

- According to one site, both his parents died when he was very young (IIRC it even said that his father was already dead by the time Toshizou was born). The older of his sisters also died early.

- Toshizou was raised first by his brother Kiroku, then moved to Onobu's home at the age of 11. This was also the time he lost his first job, through being disrespectful to his employer.

- I understand he got to know (the future) Kondou Isami when they were both in their late teens. Sources differ on whether he joined the Tennen Rishin ryuu at this time, and simply didn't become a *resident* student until later, or if he didn't in fact join until he was 28 (I think that's the right age).

- He lost another job at 17, this time he was working at a clothing store, and got fired for sleeping with one of his female co-workers. It was after this that his brother in law helped him set himself up as a medicine peddler.

So... right, wrong, what?

Oh, and a bonus question Does anyone know much about the relationship between Hijikata and Okita Souji? Most sources I've found mention how the original Shieikan four were "like brothers"... but two people I've debated with (admitted, people who really dislike the way Shinsengumi manga/anime tend to turn those two into a couple) claim that it's "well known" that Hijikata and Okita didn't get along. Which, granted, isn't exactly antithetic to their being foster brothers - you should see my family! - but I wondered if anyone could shed some light on the topic.

Linda/Enma-Ou

Glad to have you with us, Linda! I'll answer these questions for you.

>-Full name: Hijikata Toshizou Yoshitoyo. (Anyone know if he had another
>childhood name, or if he ever changed it later in life?)

Not that I know of...he was Hijikata Toshizou Yoshitoyo pretty much all the
way throughout life.

>- He was the youngest of 6 children, and had 3 brothers and 2 sisters. The
>younger sister's name was Onobu (she later married Sato Hikogorou). I read
>somewhere that the oldest brother's name was Kiroku, and the Shinsengumi!
>NHK series gives another brother's name as Tamajirou.

He was actually the youngest of nine children, although maybe only six
survived to adulthood...Onobu's original name was Otoku-- and she indeed
married Satou Hikogorou, who was Toshizou's first cousin. The siblings I
have the names of are Kiroku, Tamejirou, Daisaku, Shuu (maybe I'm reading
the kanji wrong-- it's the same "Shuu" as in "Kondou Shuusuke"), and
Onobu/Otoku.

>- According to one site, both his parents died when he was very young (IIRC
>it even said that his father was already dead by the time Toshizou was
>born). The older of his sisters also died early.

IIRC?

Hijikata Izaemon indeed did die before Toshizou was born. At age 5, Toshizou
lost his mother, Etsu. Following that, he was brought up by his formerly
separated (comment: might be �estranged�, but I don�t know�) elder brother
Kiroku and his wife.

>- I understand he got to know (the future) Kondou Isami when they were both
>in their late teens. Sources differ on whether he joined the Tennen Rishin
>ryuu at this time, and simply didn't become a *resident* student until
>later, or if he didn't in fact join until he was 28 (I think that's the
>right age)

I think they might've known each other in their childhood...but I'm not
sure.

>- He lost another job at 17, this time he was working at a clothing store,
>and got fired for sleeping with one of his female co-workers. It was after
>this that his brother in law helped him set himself up as a medicine
>peddler.

I can't remember who it was, but I read somewhere that Hijikata indeed
worked in a kimono shop at some point, and was kicked out for having an
affair with one of the (female) customers. Watsuki Nobuhiro-sensei
elaborates on this, using it as (or so I've heard) the historical basis for
why Shinomori Aoshi looks so much like Hijikata.

>Oh, and a bonus question Does anyone know much about the
>relationship between Hijikata and Okita Souji?

There's some stuff in this regard that I'm not quite sure on, but I'm trying
to sort...Okita's sister, Mitsu, was married to Rintarou, who was adopted
into the Okita family, although he was originally, like Kondou and Hijikata,
a farmer from Tama. His original family name was Inoue-- possibly pointing
to a family connection between Okita and Inoue Genzaburou...although I'm not
sure. Hijikata, as it's commonly known, probably did have a brotherly
relationship with Okita, though, and I think he even visited him in the
hospital in Edo before the Shinsengumi retreated to Aizu.

If there's anything else you'd like to know, please don't hesitate to ask.

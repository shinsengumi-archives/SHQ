Bit of a timeline regarding the retreat from Kyoto

by shimazuryu

Soo...I was checking the book "Shinsengumi Saitou Hajime no Subete" today,
and I turned up some details regarding the retreat from Kyoto. The bit below
is my combination of pages 96-97, as well as information I've previously
posted here about Kondou and Nagai Naoyuki.

Okay, so Tokugawa Yoshinobu resigns on the 9th of December, 1867. On the
evening of the 12th, Yoshinobu, Matsudaira Katamori, and Matsudaira Sadaaki
leave for Osaka. The Shinsengumi gets assigned to guard duty at Nijo
Castle-- and while there, gets the Mito domain annoyed. Thus, rather
conveniently-- Nagai Naoyuki gets ready to leave for Osaka, and takes the
Shinsengumi with him on the 14th. Shinsengumi sets up temporary headquarters
in Osaka at the Tenmangu Shrine, and on the 15th, is ordered to duty at the
Fushimi-bugyosho (Fushimi Magistrate's Headquarters), near Kyoto, in
Fushimi.

SO...to make a long story short-- Yoshimura Kanichiro (of "Mibugishiden"
fame) and Yamazaki Susumu go on ahead of the main force to set things up in
Fushimi, and the main body of Shinsengumi arrives on the 16th.

While Shinsengumi was there, Kondou continued to make business trips to Nijo
Castle. However, on the 18th, on his way back, he was attacked and shot by
the remnants of Ito Kashitaro's Goryoeji faction, under Abe Juurou. Thus,
Kondou was sent to Osaka and cared for by the Shogun's physician, Matsumoto
Ryojun (who later went to Aizu). As a result, Hijikata was the one who
commanded Shinsengumi at the Battle of Toba-Fushimi.

-M.

<<>>









As I understand from summaries and the KH mailing list, the little girl,
Yuki, is NOT Okita's daughter. She is the child of Kohana, a geisha
that Okita knows. Yuki's father is a samurai and would not/could not
marry Kohana because of her status. Yuki actually thinks of Okita as
her father as he helps take care of her and her mother.

Viz has been doing a good job oh KH so far in Shojo Beat - the first
four chapters were preceded by articles outlining the history of the
Shinsengumi and of Japan during the period; and it even made the cover
of the November issue with an interview with the Watanabe Taeko and a
"get this look" article, which even outlined how to make an
approximation of a traditional women's hairstyle! I can't speak much to
the accuracy of the translation, however, but it seems in line with
other translations I have seen.

The first collected volume should be out around New Years with the first
five chapters, it will retail for $8.99 in the US.

Viz's site on KH: http://shojobeat.com/manga/kh/

-SafetyGirl

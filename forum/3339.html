{OT} [E-book Rec.] SAMURAI CAPTIVE

by secretarytocapt3

Author Barbara Sheridan will take reader's into an exotic world of
courtesan's and samurai in 19th century Japan where a helpless British
woman is held prisoner by a mysterious warrior.

Ofcourse, if SAMURAI CAPTIVE was that simple, and the female
protagonist weepy and "helpless", I probably wouldn't bother the list
with this off-topic post.

Samurai Captive is about perception, both cultural and physical, it's
deadly mixture with lust and a high stakes game involving the opium trade.

This e-book opens with the Richardson Incident (The Namamugi Incident)
during which our feisty heroine Hannah is injured and later sold to
the Yoshiwara red-light district in Edo. Hannah not only enters into
dangerous surroundings but also finds herself in the middle of an
unlikely love triangle between two samurai.

Let's just say the other man in the story is not too happy with his
lover's newly acquired "barbarian" pet.

Overall this is a very strong work that is both romantic and thrilling
at the same time.

I leave you with a quote from the ever hard to please Mrs. Giggles, a
well known reviewer of romance novels:

Hannah's "free show leads to her being bought by one of the men in the
audience. ...Hannah is not a happy property and she bites too, not
that Katsu is too unhappy about that."
quoted from http://www.mrsgiggles.com/ebooks/sheridan_captive.html

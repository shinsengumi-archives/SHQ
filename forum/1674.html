I have the first four volumes of it. Actually, the
name of the manga is "Getsumei Seiki" ("tsuki akiraka
ni hoshi mare naki"), and the subtitle is "sayonara
Shinsengumi". Currently being published in Young
Sunday.

The main character is Hijikata Toshizo (as usual).

The story itself uses plenty of well-known fictional
clichees taken from various sources, like the fight
between Hijikata and Sakamoto Ryoma in the Genbukan
(seen also in Bakumatsu Fuunroku Makoto), the death of
Niimi Nishiki assisted by Harada Sanosuke (100% Moeyo
Ken) and so on.

I guess there are two interesting things that I
haven't seen so often:
1) Okita Souji is not a bishonen. He isn't ugly
either, but he isn't so handsome. And his eyes are
very slant, like the description left of him.
2) Saito Hajime *is* an Aizu spy working for
Matsudaira Katamori and leading a number of ninjas.
And he has a scar on his face.

The art is quite good. The story, though not
breathtaking, is reasonably interesting. I am buying
only the tankobons, so I don't know the latest
developments of the story, but the last part I have is
the night prior to the murder of Serizawa Kamo (no,
not me :P)


Serizawa Kamo

Shinsengumi in which Toba-Fushimi war was lost has returned to Edo
by ship on January 15, 1868.
(This date is the thing of an old Japanese calender.)
They stayed at a hotel called Kamaya on the first day.
Okita and Kondou were hospitalized in the hospital of Tokugawa.
(Okita has tuberculosis. Kondou was shot by the member of the Itou
group and his shoulder was injured.)
And they stayed with a samurai called Akizuki Ukyounosuke.
From February 15 to the 25th, they guarded Tokugawa Yoshinobu who
has behaved himself (kinsin).

The Tokugawa government ordered them to protect Koufu castle.
This was an artifice for driving out inconvenient Shinsengumi from
Edo to reconciliation of new government army and Tokugawa.
(Or there is an opinion that there was a secret contract between
Shinsengumi and Tokugawa government official.)

They organized the army for going to Koufu.
This is Kouyouchinbutai.
Kondou changed the name into Okubo Yamato.
Hijikata changed to Naitou Hayato.
They left Edo on March 1.
They marched through the Koushu Highway (Kousyukaidou) toward Koufu.
On the way to Kofu, there was Tama area which is their hometown.
People of their hometown welcomed their army.
The party was held in many villages along which they passed.
Their pace became late and the schedule was overdue.
Furthermore, the mountain path which goes to Koufu from Busyu was
heavy snow.

To the contrary, the new government army was very quick.
Before Kouyouchinbutai arrived there, the new government army went
into the Koufu castle.
The new government army had more soldiers and arms than they.
Hijikata returned for demanding reinforcements to Edo.
However, the battle started on March 6 before Hijikata returns.
The result was the crushing defeat of Kouyouchinbutai.
They escaped to Edo.
When they returned to Edo, Nagakura and Harada resisted Kondou and
went away.
Furthermore, many soldiers escaped.

Kondou and Hijikata made their army stay at a place called
Goheishinden (March 13).
Goheishinden is present Adachi-ku, Tokyo.
They hid true character and stayed at Kaneko's residence which is
the influential person of this place.
Kondo read books in this place.
Hijikata did fishing on the river.

Episode which Miss.B may like ^^;;;
While Hijikata is doing fishing on the river, all the girls of the
village have gathered.
They stood on the bank and saw Hijikata.
They did not know that the man was Hijikata of Shinsengumi.
Girls gathered for seeing that the very handsome samurai is doing
fishing.
"A nice man" They gossiped.


They moved the army to Nagareyama on April 2.
However, their transfer was known by the new government army which
was staying in Kasukabe.
They were surrounded by the new government army on April 3.
Kondou reported to the new government army as Tokugawa's samurai's
Okubo Yamato.
(However, his true character has become clear immediately.)
They were disarmed by the new government army and they escaped to
Aizu on April 4.
Hijikata infiltrated into Edo, in order to save Kondou.
Edo was governed by the new government army and was in the very
dangerous state for him.
Hijikata visited Katsu Kaishuu and requested rescue of Kondou.
Hijikata made the petition write to Katsu.
However, Hijikata's efforts were useless.
Kondou was executed in Itabashi on April 25.

Hijikata joined Tokugawa's army on April 10.
They gathered in a place called Kokufudai and composed the army
which made Ootori Keisuke the top on April 12.
They were divided into three groups and aimed at Aizu.

I'm sorry for the ability not performing detailed explanation.
It is difficult for me to explain in English.

In fact, I do not understand this question. (Maria-san gomennasai)
Seeing the answer of M-san, I understood somehow.

Okage de totemo wakariyasukatta desu. M-san.

Actually, the whole story as it is generally told could well be a
lie. We know that Yamanami died, but it is not clear -why- he died.
This information was orginally posted by Momoiro-usagi on the SHQ mb
if I recall correctly...

The only source which mentions "Akesato" is the work of a researcher
named Shimozawa Kan. No one can find any data relating to her outside
of his papers.

It's the same situation with the "Five Dreaded Articles". The rules
that were made after the Ikeda-ya Affair are real enough, but there
is a lot of debate over whether the first five existed. I think it
has been mentioned before that if they did exist, they were not as
harshly enforced as legend would have us believe.

It is also not clear whether or not there was actually any bad blood
between Hijikata and Yamanami. And several different versions of his
death have been given:

-He was caught after he deserted because he wanted to die.
-He wanted his death to be a warning to Kondo.
-He was not happy about the Shinsengumi being subordinates of the
Shogun and abandoning the "original" purpose of Sonno-joi.
-That he was actually seriously wounded in a fight with an enemy.
-That he killed himself because he was suffering from some sort of
neurosis.

I believe that was the gist of what we were told? Anyone have any new
info about this?

-MissB

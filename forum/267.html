by tatchan1024

Hello,

Actually, the name of the series is "Musashi no Ken"
(Sword of Musashi). It started out as a manga, and the
first story arc was made into an anime. It follows a
boy named Musashi (written with the kanji for 3, 6,
and 4) from his birth until young adulthood, as he
learns kendo and tries to become the top kendo
practitioner in Japan.

There is one flashback scene (to the Showa era) in
which a swordsman named Yamamoto Chujiro is practicing
tsuki (thrust) on an empty can hung by a string. One
day, an old man tries to do it. The can doesn't even
move, so Yamamoto thinks he missed. But when Yamamoto
takes a second look at the can...he sees that the old
man had pierced a hole in the can without even moving
it. It turns out that the old man was none other than
Saitou Hajime himself. :)

As for whether this has anything to do with the real
historical Saitou, I doubt it, but I was amused to
find yet another reference to him in a manga or anime
I've read/seen.

Tatsuko

The author of the manga "MUSASHI NO KEN", Motoka Murakami also wrote a
manga called "RON" you can view scans and translations
http://www.geocities.com/studentkendo/ron.htm

Nobuhiro Watsuki seems to be similar to Motoka Murakami, both manga
artists/writers skillfully incorporate historical figures into their
fictional works...if you read the scans above you'll find out that the
hero is defeated by a man, Takaharu Naito (he looks almost exactly the
way he was portrayed in the manga as he does in real life), who was
formerly a kendo instructor in the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Dept.

Shin-nen akemashite omedetougozaimasu.^_^
(In Japanese, it is the meaning of "A happy new year".If it
translates literally, it will become "the new year, dawn,
furthermore congratulations".)

In fact, Japan is still the daytime on December 31 now.
However, I separate from a network for about five days after this.
Therefore, I greet you previously.

Japanese people send the postcard called "Nengajyou" (New Year's
card) to friends.
It arrives on January 1.
I send Nengajyou to everybody of SHQ similarly.^_^

swordsmiths...)

As for the Shinsengumi, read that Shinsengumi manga by
Golgo 13, I mean, Saito Takao. It is reasonable. Also,

some guts to come up with such a nonsense story about
the group!

I think that Yoshida Shoin is the teacher of an ideal.
He had the capacity to find the hidden talent which all people have.
His thought attracted young students and made them consider the
future of Japan.
In fact, most of famous Ishin-Shishis of Choshuu are the students of
Shoukason-juku.
Takasugi Shinsaku, Yoshida Toshimaro, and Kusaka Genzui are the
excellent students to whom Yoshida Shoin was looking forward to the
future.
Moreover, the first Prime Minister's Itou Hirofumi was the student
here.
And Katsura Kogorou was the student of Shoin and was also a friend.

Choshuu had a grudge against the Tokugawa government from Sekigahara
war (A.D.1600).
They were deceived by Tokugawa and lost many territories.
(The Mouri family who is the daimyo of Chosyuu held the ceremony of
a curse secretly to Tokugawa on January 1 every year.)
However, I think that the reason in which the students of Shoukason-
juku hated Tokugawa is that the Tokugawa government executed Shoin
for the unreasonable reason.

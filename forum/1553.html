Toshiyoshi/Toshinaga Kawaji's secret to hunting down "bad subjects" or
the prevention of crime...
"Koe naki ni kiki, ktachi naki ni miru" = hearing voiceless voices and
seeing formless forms

From Kesatsu Shugen the policeman's textbook prepared by
Toshiyoshi/Toshinaga Kawaji "Policemen should not sleep nor sit
comfortable. Rather, they should tiptoe without rest day and night"
"[their tasks are] to endure the unendurable and bear the unbearable,
and accomplish the things that ordinary people cannot accomplish."

I have always believed that the police system was tailored for the
former samurai and indeed the article confirms this....
Toshiyoshi/Toshinaga Kawaji= police = dry nurse for the people, or an
expression of "jin" = benevolence a samurai ideal

Those of you interested in the Nagaoka domain...see page 160

Want to learn more about Hokkaido see page 223 ONWARDS....
[Hijikata devotees know this stuff...just bringing this up for the new
folks]

Hokkaido was regarded as free from political control...conjured up
utopian images "The Palace of the Dragon King" ryugu no onchi or the
Buddhist Paradise

Hokkaid=Ezoshi "a place for Ezo"
Ezo=people who are not "subject" >>> The Ainu

***The articles goes into great detail about the prison system****

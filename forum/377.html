They were the group of 200 or so samurai who joined up to go to Kyoto to
help the Shogunate quell the unrest/violence. The one backing/paying
them I believe was a Matsudaira can't-remember-just-now (not Katamori,
his father or grandfather)

Kiyokawa, the guy Sasaki is always pissed at just last week on the show
tried to slip past everyone in the rules and regulations that they are
not working for the Shogunate but "supporting the Emperor" i.e.siding
with the Choshu rebels. The bookish/smarmy Yamanami has caught this bit
of fine print and went to Sasaki with it and that's pretty much where it
left off. The synopsis for this week says:

<<Since Hachiro Kiyokawa’s plan to make Roshigumi serve for the Imperial
Court, instead of the Shogunate is revealed, the leaders of Roshigumi,
including Tadasaburo Sasaki decides to take the members back to Edo.
........ Keisuke Yamanami brings news that Roshigumi will be sent back
to Edo. Toshizo Hijikata suggests Isami to form another Roshigumi group
to stay in Kyoto. >>

You can read this synopses at the link below:
http://www.nhk-jn.co.jp/wp/best_e.htm

There's a cool little side story where the daughter of the Yagi's (where
Kondou & Kamo's groups are staying has to disguise herself as a boy so
as not to draw attention from the miscreants invading their home. Kondou
& Hijikata come upon her and apparently make comments about her being
pretty puny to wield a sword. They mention the Tennen Rishin style and
I'm guessing they said "We'll teach it to you and make a man out of
you!" The poor girl runs crying to her mother the minute the guys walk
away. It was hilarious to see the girl's look of terror as they're
lifting her arms,squeezing her muscles, adjusting the sword she's
carrying in her belt.

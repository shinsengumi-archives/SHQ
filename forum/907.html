Media news!

by bsher213

I have some very interesting information passed on to me by one of our
more quiet members Tama, (originally from Tokyo, btw)
# # # # # #
"There is a new anime series now broadcasted in Japan called "Shura no
toki"based on a manga of same title.

I don't know very much about the series, and it's not only focused on
Shinsengumi.
But I know that the story talks about a special martial arts technique
transmitted in the northern region of Mutsu( or Ohshu) and it's succsesors.

There is three parts of story which takes place in different periods.

First part is related with Musashi Miyamoto, second with Yagyu Jubee,
and the third one with Okita Souji (in Bakumatsu section).

From infomation of official site,
http://www.tv-tokyo.co.jp/anime/syuratoki/, now the section of Yagyu
Jubee is broadcasted, so Bakumatsu section will be coming soon.

Other thing; Jiro Asada the author of "Mibugishiden" published his new
novel on Shinsengumi, called "Wachigaiya Itosato".

The story talks about the mistery of assassination of Serizawa Kamo.
Heroine is Itosato, a geiko in Wachigaiya who fall in love with Hijikata.

As the author is very populer in Japan, I expect that this novel would
be adapted on the screen like Mibugishiden.
http://www.amazon.co.jp/exec/obidos/ASIN/4163229507/ref=sr_aps_b_/250-3197124-5159453"
# # # # #

How's _that_ for very cool Shinsengumi related information?

And you all thought I was just the resident hopeless fangirl. LOL

Oh yes, the world definitely NEEDS another film featuring Koichi Sato
and the still unknown (to me) wonderful actor who played Nagakura.

Oh, and Tama was kind enough to send me a brief clip of the "Hijikata
sings" scene from last week's NHK show and it appears that the song in
question is a traditional folk song from the region where
Kondou/Hijikata grew up.

--
Barbara Sheridan

http://www.barbarasheridan.net

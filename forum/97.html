Re: Hi! I'm new here . . .

by shimazuryu

The Boshin war started at the Battle of Toba-Fushimi and ran all the
way until the fall of the Goryokaku Fort in Hakodate (Hokkaido). As
far as I remember, it ran from January 8th, 1868 (the Battle of
Toba-Fushimi) to 27 June 1869, with the fall of Hakodate.

Hope that helps. If there's anything else, don't hesitate to ask.

--Masayoshi

Re: Hi! I'm new here . . .

by spiritus_saitou

Think of it this way... the BAKUmatsu was the war before Toba-
Fushimi when the shogun (the BAKUfu) was still in power and the
BoSHIN is after Toba-Fushimi when the Imperialists (iSHIN Shishi)
took power & became the Meiji Government. The stresses are put in
for emphasis to aid in remembering. Sometimes stupid little things
like that come in handy... like on tests. :-)

phil

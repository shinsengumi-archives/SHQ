*please get the free adobe acrobat reader because pdf files allow
you to zoom in on text, images and it keeps everything nice and neat

*I scanned the book backwards because I thought it would be like
most Japanese books...wrong...so the first maps you see will take
place near the [end] of the war amongst hills and mountain passes
(recall the newspaper article which mentions Fujita Goro)...as you
progress towards the beginning of the war you will see how all the
fighting was around more populated/urban areas (this is why the
government ended up burning people's homes...more on that in the
report)...to get a good general overview go to map #3 (the maps are
numbered do not use the numbering system in the pdf file---use the
numbers the author actually wrote ON the maps)

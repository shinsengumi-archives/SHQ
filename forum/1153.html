I think it means hanshi (or hanshin) of Nagaoka han, because the term
hatamoto and gokenin are reserved for Shogun's direct retainers.
After googling on the net, I couldn't find out any list of Nagaoka hanshi.

May be you already knew these sites, but I put some adresses.

A Nagaoka han's site with some details about Boshin War and famous local army
http://park3.wakwak.com/~srt/n_heisei.html

About Tsugunosuke Kawai ( his name is pronounced Tsugunosuke as I know)
http://www.geocities.co.jp/SilkRoad-Lake/6618/honmon/15.html

A war memorial for Nagaoka Hanshi in Honkoji temple
http://www.city.aizuwakamatsu.fukushima.jp/j/yukari/shiseki/nhanshihi.html

These are Japanese indication sites, so if you have the question about the
contents, you are wellcome.

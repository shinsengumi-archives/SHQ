I think this poll is a great idea.
Due to the popularity of the Shinsengumi pop culture they are now in
many languages. Like our own personal struggles with online
translators such as http://www.nifty.com/globalgate which at best can
help us locate interesting text (so that we can forward the info to
volunteer HUMAN translators), new fans in other countries will be
faced with similar difficulties.

Perhaps we can, over time come, up with a standard info sheet only 1-3
paragraphs long which include the most basic Shinsengumi information.
Translate it into other languages, post in the files section so that
new fans will have a basic foundation from which to build their new
fansites. The info sheet should be as neutral as possible to allow
new fans from different cultural backgrounds to begin their research
without being exposed to too much bias right away.

An great example of how the Shinsengumi have been "translated" can be
seen here
http://members.thai.net/peacemakerdiary/
see this page for an image of a map to Ikedaya in Thai
http://members.thai.net/peacemakerdiary/ikedaya.htm
Nong Giffy, you are a member here if you need assistance on the Saitou
page please email me off-list at secretarytocap3(a)hotmail.com---I
emailed you already (^_^)

When a manga is translated---footnotes are included---footnotes which
are highly biased or simply innaccurate.

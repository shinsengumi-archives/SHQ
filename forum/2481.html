As you may know there is more than one Dai-bosatsu Toge (based on
the novel of the same name) the most popular and easy to find is the
one starring Tatsuya Nakadai directed by Kihachi Okamoto...the other
version is called Satan's Sword directed by Kenji Misumi, which
sticks to the novel more which means it will cover more ground
during the Shinsengumi "arc". Satan's Sword is in the process of
being re-subbed right now and will be sold unofficially.

for our new members the novel (which is SUPER hard to find in
English) can be downloaded
https://mywebspace.wisc.edu/tnamwong/web/Dai-bosatsu%20Toge/DBT.zip
6mb
even if you don't have time to read it -now- just please download
while I can still host the file---when the novel discusses Shimabara
the author kind of goes off on a tangent but he does provide some
valuable info

Shogun's Most Dreaded Samurai Corps: The Bloody Legacy Of The
Shinsengumi (Hardcover)
by Romulus Hillsborough
someone went to a bookstore to inquire about the status of this book
and was told that Tuttle Publishing "dropped" this book---which
doesn't mean that it well never ever be published but simply this
company won't do it---there goes our solitary beacon of historical
info on the Shinsengumi in *ENGLISH*

------------------------------------
Very cool 7 page article
"Lost in History: Aizu and the Meiji Restoration"
https://mywebspace.wisc.edu/tnamwong/web/LostInHistoryAizu.pdf
please pay really close attention to pages 6-7
MANY texts especially those written in the first half of the 20th
century emphasize how *peaceful* the Restoration/1868 was
similarly, the special enthronement book of Emperor Hirohito 1928 did
not place any emphasis on 1868 (it was simply the Tokugawa Shogunate
and Meiji Era)---when one reads the section on Kyoto's history there
is no mention of any "conflict" at all

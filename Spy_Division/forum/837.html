[notes on Jozai Han by Hirotada Tokugawa]
The ancestors of Hayashi Tadataka, daimyo of the Jozai domain, hunted
for rabbits as a new year's dish for Nitta Yoshisada, the ancestor of
Tokugawa Ieyasu. Soon after, the Hayashi family began serving a son of
the Nitta who was the founder of the family which became the Tokugawa.

From then on, the head of the Hayashi family would present preserved
rabbit meat to the head of the Matsudaira (later known as Tokugawa)
family on new year's day, and in return, the head of the Hayashi
family had the right to receive his new year's sake cup before any
other retainer of the Matsudaira/Tokugawa clan.

Because of this, the Hayashi family's "triple-tomoe" crest
(http://www.kanjistyle.com/gif/JFC003.gif) had the kanji for "one" or
"first" beneath it-- which created what was later known as the "Jozai
tomoe," after the Hayashi family's fief
(http://www.asahi-net.or.jp/~me4k-skri/han/kamon/jouzai-hayasi-tomoe1.jpg)

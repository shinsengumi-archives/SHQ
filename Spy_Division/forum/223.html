That's why quoting the source (this ML) is the wiser thing to do, to avoid all the nasty thing of leading unnecessary mass charges.
Since the 'accuser party' is not a seer in most cases , and unless the accused one quotes the source previously, the accuser is absolutely entitled to suspicion, and I mean the historical data in this case. Sorry if I sound stubborn.

Honestly, if I find that any of my author notes is taken by someone else (comma by comma), and this person doesn't quote the source, I simply rip the guy/gal to bits. He/she can come with friends and protest all they like, but unless they provide real and solid proof, I simply don't back off, no matter how many charge (and believe me, I know, I faced mass charges). But, maybe it's just me...

It's the strict responsibility of the author to be clear about this, and avoid uncomfortable situations. It doesn't take too long to mention: thanks to the gals and guys in the SHQ ML, who provided ideas and historical data.

Concerning the plotlines, the right thing is, if you know a previous fic with a similar idea, to contact the author. It's also simple and quick, and avoid misunderstandings.
